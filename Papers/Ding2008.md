## Abstract

Comprehensive experimental comparison of time-series representations and distance measures on the UCR benchmark. The landmark finding is that 1-NN DTW is **extremely difficult to beat** across virtually all datasets — establishing it as the canonical baseline for any time-series classification task.

---

## Key Contributions

- Systematic comparison of 8 distance measures × multiple representations on UCR datasets
- Famous result: **1-NN DTW with optimal warping window ≈ best-in-class** for most datasets
- Debunked claims of various representations beating DTW when evaluated fairly

## Results & Findings

- No representation or distance measure consistently outperforms 1-NN DTW
- DTW with a properly tuned Sakoe-Chiba window achieves state-of-the-art on most UCR benchmarks of the time

## Critical Analysis

**Strengths:**
- Rigorous, reproducible benchmark methodology
- Definitive reference for justifying DTW as the baseline

**Limitations:**
- Focused on univariate time series; gesture recognition uses multivariate (3D coordinates)
